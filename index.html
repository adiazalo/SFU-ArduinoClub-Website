SFU Arduino Club's Journey

  • October 2013

    Our Humble Beginnings

    In Fall of 2013, Kush Chhatbar and Tomoe Yoshihara stumbled into each other in Engineering Graphics and Design course. We instantly became good friends due to our passion for technology and building things. Later that day we decided to meet after class so that Tomoe could teach Kush how to learn Arduino Programming. Thats when it all began!

  • January 2014

    A CLUB was formed

    After learning how to work with Arduino and the potential it has in helping one prototype systems quickly. We thought to ourselves, maybe there are other students out there who would like to learn Arduino and collaborate with its open source community of people? Well, there it goes again! That's when we officially rolled the red carpet and started calling our close friends to join our club. We were rollin'.

  • February - December 2014

    Transition to FULL Service

    With all that passion and excitement to teach and introduce everyone with what we could do with Arduino, the sky was the limit! During this period, we had many of our members working on their own or group projects and we went on and on doing so, until we changed things a little bit to pick up even more momentum and this time grants for our club members' projects. (remember to check them out in our Projects section!)

  • January 2015 - Present

    Phase Two Expansion

    It's just getting better!. We now have numerous large projects of which some are almost complete and others are underway. We have more grants coming in, new members joining in, we have moved from SFU Surrey campus to a new space at Maker Cube thanks to Dylan (who by the way, was one of our earliest members when we started Arduino Club). Oh and now we even have a co-op student working with all the communication related aspects of the club! How cool is that! With great projects, great retreats and great people; we are not only a club. We are a team!

Audrey - Plant Monitoring

Ethnorobotonics

Ethnorobotanics is an automated, intelligent, and customizable system to water a garden. It is also web enabled for data logging purposes. The ‘robotonist’ will water the plant according to a user- defined soil humidity threshold which is set remotely through a web portal/mobile application.

We aim to facilitate the gardening process by allowing gardeners to work on a flexible schedule with decreased risk of crops suffering from lack of regular watering. We believe in developing accessible, affordable, and easily repairable technology for aspiring gardeners who are presently unable to care for crops on a regular basis. Lots of people want to garden, but not everybody can commit the time for watering; let’s get a robot to do it!
